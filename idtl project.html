Health Impact of Food Adulteration

‚ö†Ô∏è Why Food Adulteration is Dangerous

Food adulteration is the practice of adding inferior, harmful, or unnecessary substances to food items to increase quantity and profit. It poses serious health risks and can lead to chronic diseases.

üè• Common Health Issues Caused by Adulterants:

  • Digestive Problems: Stomach pain, diarrhea, food poisoning
  • Organ Damage: Kidney and liver damage from chemicals
  • Cancer: Carcinogenic dyes and chemicals
  • Neurological Issues: Brain damage from heavy metals
  • Respiratory Problems: Asthma and breathing difficulties
  • Skin Diseases: Allergies and skin infections

‚ò†Ô∏è Deadly Adulterants and Their Effects:

  • Metanil Yellow (in sweets): Causes cancer, liver damage
  • Argemone Oil (in mustard oil): Epidemic dropsy, heart failure
  • Urea (in milk): Kidney problems, acidity
  • Lead Chromate (in turmeric): Brain damage, anemia
  • Sudan Dyes (in spices): Cancer-causing agent
  • Formalin (in fish/milk): Cancer, asthma, skin diseases

üõ°Ô∏è Prevention Tips:

  • Buy food from licensed and reputable vendors
  • Check FSSAI license on food packages
  • Perform simple home tests for common adulterants
  • Report suspected adulteration to authorities
  • Educate family and friends about food safety
  • Support organic and local farmers
  • Don't compromise on food quality for lower prices
